Interoch Numbers Theory: A Comprehensive Framework

The Interoch Numbers Theory explores sets of integers closed under specific digit-wise operations. If \( S \) is such a set and \( o \) is the operation, then for every \( n \in S \), applying \( o \) to its digits yields another number in \( S \).

Foundational Definitions

Let d(n) denote the digits of \( n \). Closure means \( o(d(n)) \in S \) for all \( n \in S \).

Key Types of Interoch Sets

  • Addinteroch – Sum of digits. Example: 58 → 5 + 8 = 13.
  • Subinteroch – Absolute difference of digits. Example: 94 → |9 − 4| = 5.
  • Mulinteroch – Product of digits. Example: 39 → 3 × 9 = 27.
  • Neutinteroch – Mix of addition and subtraction. Example: 47 → (4 + 7) − |4 − 7| = 11 − 3 = 8.
  • Codeinteroch – Digit shift transformations. Example: 123 → shift +2 → 345.
  • Swappinteroch – Swap positions of digits. Example: 73 → 37.
  • Splitinteroch – Split digits into groups and operate. Example: 123 → 1 + 23 = 24.
  • Groupinteroch – Group and transform digits flexibly. Example: 1242 → 12 + 42 = 54.
